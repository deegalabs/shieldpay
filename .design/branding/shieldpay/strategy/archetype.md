# Archetype

> Phase: strategy | Brand: ShieldPay | Generated: 2026-06-27

---

## Primary: The Guardian / Steward

A blend rooted in the Caregiver (protect what is entrusted) crossed with the
Ruler (order, exactness, custody of the books). ShieldPay is the party who holds
the sensitive number, keeps it private, keeps the records clean, and can prove
the books are right when asked. Trust is earned through restraint and care, not
through bravado.

This is the right archetype because it inverts what the privacy-anxious CFO fears
about crypto. The crypto category reads as Outlaw or Magician (disruption,
spectacle, "trust the protocol"). ShieldPay reads as the steward who would never
publish your payroll, never lose a record, and never ask you to take its word for
it. The Guardian protects the secret; the Steward keeps the ledger exact. The
indigo-to-emerald gradient already encodes both halves: indigo holds and reassures,
emerald confirms and settles.

## Core traits

- **Protective.** The default posture toward a salary is "mask it." Privacy is not
  a setting the user has to find; it is the resting state of the product.
- **Custodial / exact.** Keeps clean books. Figures are tabular, aligned, and
  precise to the cent. A steward who is sloppy with numbers is not a steward.
- **Calm under scrutiny.** A Guardian does not panic when an auditor or a court
  shows up. ShieldPay invites verification; it does not fear it.
- **Discreet.** Says only what is needed. Withholds the salary by design and
  discloses deliberately. Quiet is a feature, not an absence of personality.
- **Accountable.** Stands behind every payment with a proof, not a promise. The
  Guardian takes responsibility for being checkable.

## How it shows up in the product

- **Private by default.** Amounts arrive masked. The contributor and the public
  see "paid" and "verified," not the figure. Revealing is an explicit, logged act.
- **Disclosure is a deliberate handover.** Granting an auditor access feels like
  handing over a key for a fixed window: scoped, time-boxed, revocable, read-only.
  The product makes the boundary visible, never accidental.
- **The verified badge is the recurring reassurance.** Across all three portals,
  the emerald "verified" moment is the Guardian saying "this is correct, and you
  can check it yourself." It is the emotional payoff of the whole product.
- **Nothing is exposed carelessly.** No salary in clear text, no balance shown to
  the auditor, no other company's data ever in view. Containment is structural.

## How it shows up in voice

Calm, precise, trustworthy (developed fully in voice-and-tone.md). The Guardian
speaks in short, exact sentences, states figures plainly, never hypes, never
alarms. It reassures by being specific: "Verified on-chain" beats "ultra-secure."
It takes responsibility in errors rather than blaming the user.

## How it shows up in visuals

(Confirming the discover/mood-board direction, not re-deriving it.)

- **Depth and containment** — layered dark slate surfaces that "hold" the secret;
  elevation by surface-lightness, not flashy shadow. The Guardian's calm is built,
  not decorated.
- **The masked → verified → disclosed motif** as the signature visual language: a
  deliberate masked chip (designed, not a broken blur), an emerald verified badge,
  and a scoped reveal for the auditor.
- **The indigo→emerald gradient as dimensional light** — protected, then proven.
  Used as atmosphere on key surfaces, never as 2019 text fill.
- **Tabular and mono figures everywhere** — the Steward's exactness, made visible
  on every amount, hash, and proof ID.

## What the Guardian/Steward is NOT

- **Not the Outlaw.** No "burn down the banks," no rebellion, no crypto swagger.
  ShieldPay is the calm professional in the room, not the disruptor.
- **Not the Magician.** No "magic," no spectacle, no "trust the black box." The
  proof is the opposite of magic: it is checkable. We never ask for faith.
- **Not the Hero.** ShieldPay is not the protagonist. The CFO, contributor, and
  auditor are. The Guardian works quietly behind their decisions, not on a podium.
- **Not the Caregiver's soft, warm extreme.** No hand-holding, no "we've got you
  bestie," no illustrated friendliness (that is Deel's lane). The care here is
  expressed as rigor and discretion, not warmth.
- **Not the Sage lecturing.** We do not teach the CFO cryptography. "Cryptography
  is invisible" means the Guardian carries the knowledge so the user never has to.

## Shadow traits to avoid

- **Over-protectiveness that becomes friction** — privacy must not make the
  product feel locked, slow, or hard to use. Guard the data, not the user.
- **Cold rigidity** — exactness should read as trustworthy, not bureaucratic or
  punishing. Precise, not severe.
- **Paternalism** — never imply the user cannot be trusted with their own data or
  cannot understand their own books. The Guardian serves; it does not gatekeep.
