# Messaging

> Phase: strategy | Brand: ShieldPay | Generated: 2026-06-27

---

## Core message

**Private payroll you can prove.** Pay contributors in stablecoins, keep each
salary private by default, and prove every payment correct on-chain, disclosed only
to the auditor you choose.

## Messaging pillars

Three pillars, one per word of the essence (private / provable / on your terms),
plus the invisible-crypto promise underneath all three.

### Pillar 1 — Private by default

The salary never appears in clear text on-chain. A watcher sees that a payment
happened, not how much. Privacy is the resting state, not a setting to find.

- **Proof point:** No plaintext amount is ever stored or sent. Only the commitment
  and the agreed range are recorded, so there is nothing to leak or scrape.
- **Honest limit (stated, not hidden):** the *fact* of a payment between two
  parties is public on Stellar. We hide the amount, not the existence. We say so.

### Pillar 2 — Proven, not promised

Every payment carries a proof anyone can check on-chain, that it was correct and
within the agreed range, without trusting ShieldPay.

- **Proof point:** Each run produces a proof verified inside a smart contract on
  Stellar; the verified record is queryable by anyone, including a court or tax
  authority, with ShieldPay out of the loop.
- **Why it matters:** competitors offer an invoice and a database. ShieldPay offers
  math a third party can independently confirm.

### Pillar 3 — Disclosed on your terms

The company alone decides who sees exact figures, for which period, and for how
long. Disclosure is scoped, time-boxed, and revocable.

- **Proof point:** The auditor portal is read-only, limited to the selected
  period, and cannot move funds, see the company balance, or read other companies'
  books. Access expires on a date the company sets.

### Pillar 4 (foundation) — Cryptography stays invisible

You never read "zero-knowledge" to use it. The product speaks in "private,"
"verified," and "disclosed." The math runs underneath.

- **Proof point:** Finance-grade interface modeled on Mercury and Stripe, not on a
  block explorer. A CFO who distrusts crypto can run payroll without learning any.

## Per-persona value propositions

### Company (CFO / Ops)

**Key message:** Run payroll on-chain without publishing every salary, and prove
each payment was correct without asking anyone to trust your word.

- Pay contributors in seconds in native USDC, with fees in fractions of a cent.
- Keep each salary private; you are not posting your comp table to a public ledger.
- Hand a court or auditor a proof, not a screenshot, when a payment is questioned.
- **Tone:** confident, restrained, exact. Compares to Mercury and Deel, not crypto.
- **Proof point:** live on Stellar testnet; amounts masked, payments verifiable.

### Worker / Contributor

**Key message:** You were paid, and here is proof anyone can check, without anyone
learning your salary.

- A clear record of every payment, with a verified badge you can trust on sight.
- Your salary stays private. The proof that you were paid is yours to download.
- Easy receipt for tax filing; cash out through Stellar's existing rails.
- **Tone:** warmer, plain, reassuring. No ZK literacy required, ever.
- **Proof point:** the "verified" moment, exact figure shown only to you and those
  you allow.

### Auditor / Accountant

**Key message:** Read-only, time-boxed access to exact figures for the period you
were granted, with on-chain proofs you can independently confirm.

- Exact, aligned, tabular figures and exportable reports, with accounting gravitas.
- Verify each payment on-chain yourself; you are not trusting ShieldPay's claim.
- You see only the granted period, never the balance, never other companies.
- **Tone:** precise, formal, read-only posture made explicit. Legitimacy over warmth.
- **Proof point:** access expires on a set date; export to CSV/PDF for filing.

## Elevator line

> **ShieldPay is confidential payroll for on-chain companies.** You pay
> contributors in stablecoins, each salary stays private, and every payment is
> proven correct on-chain, so anyone can confirm a payment happened without
> learning the amount. When you need to, you disclose exact figures to an auditor
> you authorize, for a fixed window. It is private payroll you can prove.

## Objection handling

| Objection | Response |
| --- | --- |
| "Isn't everything on a blockchain public?" | The fact of a payment is public; the amount is not. ShieldPay records a private commitment, never the figure. Anyone can see a payment happened; only an authorized auditor sees how much. |
| "I don't trust crypto for payroll." | You never touch the crypto. The interface is finance-grade, settlement is in native USDC, and you read "private" and "verified," never "zero-knowledge." Cryptography stays invisible. |
| "How is this 'proof' better than an invoice?" | An invoice is a claim in a vendor's database. ShieldPay's proof is checkable on-chain by anyone, including a court, without trusting us. Math, not a screenshot. |
| "What if I'm audited or sued?" | Disclose exact figures to a named auditor for a set period, read-only and revocable. The on-chain proof stands on its own even if ShieldPay disappears. |
| "Why not just use Deel?" | Deel keeps your books private but proves nothing on-chain, and it doesn't settle in stablecoins. ShieldPay adds independent provability and on-chain settlement Deel cannot. |
| "Why not a public on-chain payroll tool?" | Those publish every contributor's salary to anyone with the address. ShieldPay gives you the same on-chain settlement with the amounts kept private. |

## Quality check

Each pillar, value prop, and objection response names something specific to
ShieldPay's provable-privacy position: "private commitment, never the figure,"
"verifiable on-chain without trusting us," "read-only, revocable disclosure." Swap
a competitor's name in and the lines break, the test the brief required.
