# Brand Platform

> Phase: strategy | Brand: ShieldPay | Generated: 2026-06-27

---

## Essence

**Private, and provable.** Three words that hold the whole brand: the salary is
kept private, and the payment is proven correct. Neither half works alone.

## Purpose (Why)

We believe paying someone privately and proving you paid them correctly should be
the same act, not a trade-off. Today a company must choose between a public ledger
that exposes every salary and a private system that asks the world to take its
word. ShieldPay exists to dissolve that choice.

## Vision

A world where confidential payroll is the default for on-chain companies: where a
DAO can pay a contributor without publishing their salary, and still hand a court
or a tax authority math instead of a promise.

## Mission

Settle payroll in stablecoins on Stellar with each amount private by default,
prove every payment correct with an on-chain proof anyone can check, and let the
company disclose exact figures only to the auditors it authorizes.

## Promise

**The amount stays yours. The proof is everyone's.** What you pay a contributor is
private by default. That you paid them correctly is verifiable by anyone, on-chain,
without trusting us. And you alone decide who sees the exact figure, and for how long.

## Values (behavioral, not platitudes)

- **Mask first, reveal on purpose.** Sensitive numbers default to private. Every
  disclosure is a deliberate, scoped, logged act, never an accident or a default.
- **Prove, don't promise.** When we say a payment is correct, we ship a proof a
  third party can check, not a claim a user must believe.
- **Keep the books exact.** Figures are precise to the cent, aligned, and
  consistent everywhere. A steward who is loose with numbers is no steward.
- **Hide the cryptography, not the truth.** The user never reads "zero-knowledge."
  The math runs underneath; the truth it produces is always in plain sight.
- **Disclose the limits honestly.** We say plainly what is private (the amount) and
  what is public (that a payment occurred). We never oversell the privacy.

## The "only" statement (the one ownable claim)

> **ShieldPay is the only payroll where each salary stays private and every
> payment is proven correct on-chain, disclosed solely to the auditor you choose.**

This is the line no competitor can echo: Deel cannot say "proven correct on-chain,"
Superfluid cannot say "each salary stays private," and a block explorer cannot say
"disclosed solely to the auditor you choose." It is true to the product (live on
testnet) and specific to all three personas.

## Tagline candidates

1. **Private payroll you can prove.** (Lead pick.) Captures the whole category in
   four words, plain enough for a skeptical CFO, no crypto jargon, owns the seam.
2. **Pay privately. Prove publicly.** The masked → verified motion as a couplet;
   sharp and rhythmic for a hero.
3. **Protected, then proven.** Maps directly to the indigo→emerald gradient; best
   as an internal/visual line rather than the headline.
4. **The amount is yours. The proof is everyone's.** The promise compressed; warmer,
   good for an about page or manifesto, slightly long for a logo lockup.
5. **Salaries stay private. Payments stay provable.** The most literal; strong for
   the auditor/CFO surfaces where precision beats poetry.

**Recommendation:** lead with #1 as the primary tagline, hold #3 as the visual
motif line tied to the gradient, and use #4 for longer-form brand copy.

## Intensity dials

These calibrated dials drive the identity and design-system phases. Scale 1–5.
ShieldPay's posture is **calm + high craft, not flashy**: maximal on
precision/trust/legibility, minimal on ornamentation/hype, present-but-restrained
on motion.

| Dial | Setting | Rationale |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Boldness** | **2 / 5** | Quiet authority. The position is bold (own "provable privacy"), but the surface stays restrained. A skeptical CFO trusts the calm, not the shout. |
| **Density** (information) | **4 / 5** | Payroll and audit tables are dense by nature. Lean dense-but-legible: tabular figures, tiered text, aligned columns. Density is a legitimacy cue, not clutter. |
| **Legibility / Precision** | **5 / 5** | The Steward's exactness. Every amount, hash, and proof ID is tabular, mono, exact to the cent. This is non-negotiable and the cheapest credibility win. |
| **Motion** | **2 / 5** | Restrained and physical. Motion only confirms or reveals (the payroll stepper, the disclosure reveal). No loops, no decoration. The one place to spend it is the trust-critical confirm sequence. |
| **Warmth** | **2 / 5** | Cool and professional, not cold. Care shows as discretion and rigor, not friendliness. This is the deliberate counter to Deel's warm, illustrated register. |
| **Ornamentation** | **1 / 5** | Near-zero. No decorative flourish, no illustration, no neon. The gradient-as-light is the only expressive element, and even that reads as atmosphere, not paint. |
| **Contrast** | **4 / 5** | High, for hierarchy and WCAG AA. Lead numbers and verified states pop against deep slate; the rest recedes. Contrast carries the hierarchy that ornamentation does not. |
| **Color saturation** | **2 / 5** | Neutrals carry 90%+ of every surface. Indigo and emerald appear only as signal: protected, then proven. If a screen looks colorful, it is wrong. |

**Net for the next phases:** build the calm with craft, not decoration. Spend the
budget on depth (surface-lightness elevation), precision (tabular/mono figures),
and one disciplined confirm-motion. Hold ornamentation, warmth, and hype near the
floor. The brand should feel like a finance instrument a CFO already trusts, with
a privacy signature no competitor has.
